About me

In 2010, I moved across the country to Rochester, New York—sight unseen (and, if I'm being honest, on a bit of a whim)—to enroll in the Master of Arts in Literary Translation Studies (MALTS) program at the University of Rochester. The two years that followed—defined by inspiring faculty, laughter-filled translation workshops, and a community of weird-like-me word nerds—surpassed my expectations.

A few years after graduating from the MALTS program, on another whim, I decided to try my hand at modern programming languages. Turns out, learning JavaScript isn't entirely dissimilar from learning Spanish, or Portuguese, or Quechua. These days, when I'm not translating fiction or writing code—which is just another form of translation—I can often be found playing board games with friends or hiking in the Boise foothills.
